3 Cross product

Definition 3.1 \[\begin{align} \mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b} = (a_y \, b_z - a_z \, b_y) \, \widehat{\mathbf{i}} \end{align}\]

Notice that the cross product of two vectors is a vector.

3.1 Calculation using determinants

\[\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b} = \left\vert\begin{array}{ccc} \widehat{\mathbf{i}} & \widehat{\mathbf{j}} & \widehat{\mathbf{k}} \\ a_{x} & a_{y} & a_{z}\\ b_{x} & b_{y} & b_{z}\\ \end{array}\right|\]

Exercises

  1. [True/False] Cross product satisfies the commutative property.

  2. Show that the cross product does not satisfy the commutative property.
