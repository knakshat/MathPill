Applied Mathematics for Engineers: The Theoretical Minimum

Chapter 1 Introduction to Mathematics

“Mathematics is not merely an idle art form, it is an essential part of our society.” – Richard Hamming

Learning outcomes

This chapter provides basic symbols of mathematics, rudiments of logic, and different modes of a mathematical proof. A read of the chapter will help an engineering student to appreciate mathematical writings, and it will, hopefully, help the student to bring rigor and precision in their technical writings. Learning how to write math is an important skill to have for a researcher, as Galileo GALILEI remarked: Mathematics is the language of Science.

1.1 Mathematical symbols

We employ the following notation for the fundamental number systems: \[\begin{align*} \mathbb{N} &= \mbox{the set of positive integers} \\ \mathbb{Z} &= \mbox{the set of integers} \\ \mathbb{Q} &= \mbox{the set of rational numbers} \\ \mathbb{R} &= \mbox{the set of real numbers} \\ \mathbb{C} &= \mbox{the set of complex numbers} \end{align*}\]
